Geometric Distribution

\(X \sim Geo(p)\)

\(E(x) = \frac1p \)

\(S_{\infty} = \frac{p}{1-q} = 1 \)

\(Mode\ =\ 1\ (Always) \)

\(Median\ = -\frac{\log(2)}{\log(1-p)} \le M \le 1 - \frac{\log(2)}{\log(1-p)} \)

\(p(X = r) = p*q^{r-1} \)

Info:

Sometimes also known as the distribution of rare events. The Poisson distribution is where discrete and infinite events occur in continuous but finite intervals of time or space

Example 1

I play a game in which I need to throw a 6 to start. What is the probability that
a) I start on my fourth turn
b) I have not started after 6 turns

a)
I have to get something other than a 6 on the first three turns and then throw a 6, so the probability is \((\frac56)^3 * \frac16 = \frac{125}{1296} = 0.0965\ (3s.f.) \)

b)
I have to get something other than a 6 on all of the first siz throws, so the probability is \((\frac56)^6 = 0.335\ (3s.f.)\)

Example 2

If \(X \sim Geo(0.4)\) find the probability that \(X = 3\).

\(p(X = 3) = 0.6^2 * 0.4 = 0.144 \)

Example 3

If \(X \sim Geo(0.8)\) find \(p(X \le 5)\) correct to 4 significant figures.

\(p(X \le 5) = 1 - p(X > 5) = 1 - 0.2^5 = 0.9997\ (4s.f.)\)

Example 4

I toss a fiar coin until I see the first head. What is the probability that I need
a) exactly three tosses
b) at least five tosses

If X is the number of tosses until the first head then \(X \sim Geo(0.5)\) and I need to find \(p(X=3)\).

a)
\(p(X=3) = 0.5^2 * 0.5 = 0.125\)
b)
\(p(X \ge 5) = p(X > 4) = 0.5^4 = 0.0625\)

Example 5

If \(X \sim Geo(0.4)\) find
a) \(p(X = 2) \)     b)\(p(X > 4)\)     c)\(p(X = 6)\)     d)\(P(X = 6 | X > 4)\)

a)\(p(X = 2) = 0.6 * 0.4 = 0.24\)
b)\(p(X > 4) = 0.6^4 = 0.1296\)
c)\(p(X = 6) = 0.6^5 * 0.4 = 0.031104\)
d)\(p(X = 6 | X > 4) = \frac{p(X = 6\ and\ X > 4)}{p(X > 4)} = \frac{p(X = 6)}{p(X > 4)} = \frac{0.6^5 * 0.4}{0.6^4} = 0.6 * 0.4 = 0.24\)
