Normal Distribution

\(X \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)\)

\(\mu = population\ mean\)

\(\sigma^2 = population\ variance \)

\(p(X = r) = \frac{e^{-\lambda * \lambda^x}}{x!} \)

Info:

The Normal distribution, also known as the Gaussian distribution, is a continuous probability distribution characterized by a symmetric bell-shaped curve.

\(X \sim N(\) \(,\) \()\)

\(p(\)\()\)

Example 1

In 2014, Americans spent on average 40 minutes per day on social media. If the distribution of these times is normally distributed and the standard deviation is 9.7 mins.

1. Calculate the probability that an American spends less than 15 minutes a day on social media
\(p(X < 15)\)
\(z = \frac{30 - 40}{9.7} = -2.577 \)
\(p = 0.005\)

2. Calculate the probability that an American spends between 30 minutes and 40 minutes a day on social media
\(0.5 - p(X < 30) = p(30 < X < 40)\)
\(z = \frac{30 - 40}{9.7} = -1.031\)
\(1 - (0.5 + \Phi(z)) = 0.3487\)

Example 2

If the average distance run by a scrum half during a rugby game is 6.5km, and the distances are normally distributed and the standard deviation is 1.4km

1. Calculate the probability that a scrum half runs less than 4km
\(p(X < 4)\)
\(z = \frac{4 - 6.5}{1.4} = -1.786\)
\(\Phi(-1.786) = 0.037\)

Example 3

The amount of liquid, X, in randomly chosen bottles of water is normally distributed with mean 400ml and standard deviation of 3.3ml so \(X \sim N(400, 3.3^2)\)

Find the probability that a randomly chosen bottle contains:

1. At least 395ml
\(p(X \ge 395)\)
\(z = \frac{395 - 400}{3.3} = -1.515\)
\(\Phi(-1.515) = 0.935\)

2. More than 402ml
\(p(X > 402)\)
\(z = \frac{402 - 400}{3.3} = 0.606\)
\(\Phi(0.606) = 0.272\)

3. Between 404ml and 408ml
\(p(404 < X < 408)\)
\(z = \frac{404 - 400}{3.3} = 1.212\)
\(z = \frac{408 - 400}{3.3} = 2.424\)
\(\Phi(2.424) - \Phi(1.212) = 0.105\)

Example 4

The height in cm, H, of year 12 students from a large number of schools is recorded and it is found that the normal distribution is a good model for the heights with \(H \sim N(172, 47)\)

Find the probability that a randomly chosen student:
1. Is less than 167cm tall
\(p(X < 167)\)
\(z = -0.729\)
\(\Phi(-0.729) = 0.233\)

2. Is greater than 183cm tall
\(p(X > 183)\)
\(z = 1.605\)
\(\Phi(1.605) = 0.0543\)

3. Is between 155cm and 163cm tall
\(p(155 < X < 163)\)
\(z = -2.480\)
\(z = -1.313\)
\(\Phi(-1.313) - \Phi(-2.480)\)
\(= 0.88\)

4. If a school has 86 year 12 students, find approximately how many would be less than 180cm tall
\(p(X < 180)\)
\(z = \frac{180 - 172}{\sqrt{47}} = 1.167\)
\(\Phi(1.167) = 0.878\)
\(86 * 0.878 = \sim 76\)
